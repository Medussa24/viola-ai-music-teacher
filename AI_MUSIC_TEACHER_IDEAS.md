# AI Music Teacher App Ideas

## Core Vision

An AI-powered music teacher that listens, watches progress, explains clearly, and adapts lessons to the student. The app should feel like a patient private instructor: practical, encouraging, specific, and always ready with the next useful exercise.

## Target Learners

- Absolute beginners who need structure and confidence.
- Returning players who want guided practice without scheduling lessons.
- Intermediate students who need feedback on timing, tone, pitch, rhythm, and musicality.
- Parents helping children practice between lessons.
- Self-taught musicians who need accountability and correction.

## Main Learning Loop

1. Student chooses an instrument, skill level, goal, and practice time.
2. App assigns a focused practice session.
3. Student plays, sings, taps rhythm, or answers theory prompts.
4. AI gives immediate feedback on what improved, what needs work, and how to fix it.
5. App updates the practice plan and tracks long-term progress.

## MVP Feature Set

- Onboarding quiz for instrument, experience, goals, and practice schedule.
- Daily practice plan with warmup, technique, repertoire, and review.
- Audio recording and playback.
- Basic pitch detection feedback.
- Basic rhythm/timing feedback.
- AI lesson chat for explanations and encouragement.
- Progress dashboard with streaks, completed lessons, and weak areas.
- Exercise library sorted by skill and instrument.
- Teacher-style recap after every session.

## Signature Features

- Real-time practice coach: listens while the student plays and gives short, actionable corrections.
- Adaptive lesson path: the app changes tomorrow's lesson based on today's mistakes.
- "Explain it differently" button: turns confusing theory into simpler explanations, analogies, or examples.
- Slow practice mode: breaks difficult passages into tiny chunks and gradually increases tempo.
- Mistake pattern detection: identifies repeated issues like rushing, flat notes, uneven bowing, poor fingering, or weak counting.
- Practice accountability: reminders, goals, streaks, and weekly summaries.
- Repertoire roadmap: songs/pieces unlocked by skill level.
- Performance mode: student records a full take and receives a detailed scorecard.

## Feedback Types

- Rhythm accuracy.
- Tempo consistency.
- Note duration.
- Dynamics.
- Articulation.
- Tone quality.
- Intonation tendencies.
- Missed notes.
- Repeated hesitation points.
- Musical expression.

## AI Teacher Personality

- Patient, direct, and specific.
- Encouraging without being vague.
- Gives one or two fixes at a time.
- Avoids overwhelming beginners.
- Sounds like a real teacher, not a grading machine.
- Celebrates effort and consistency.

- 2-minute warmup.
- 5-minute technique focus.
- 5-minute rhythm or ear training.
- 10-minute repertoire work.
- 3-minute review challenge.
- End-of-session recap with tomorrow's focus.

## Technical Ideas

- Web app first for fast iteration.
- Microphone recording through browser.
- Pitch detection with Web Audio API or a library.
- Rhythm comparison against expected note timings.
- AI chat powered by lesson context and student history.
- Local-first saved recordings where possible.
- Optional cloud accounts for sync and teacher sharing.
- Future computer vision for posture, hand shape, bow angle, and fingering.

## MVP Build Path

1. Static app shell with dashboard, lesson page, recorder, and AI chat placeholder.
2. Add recording and playback.
3. Add pitch detection for single-note exercises.
4. Add rhythm/timing scoring with clapping or simple melodies.
5. Add adaptive practice recommendations.
6. Add progress history.
7. Add polished instrument-specific curriculum, starting with viola.

## Open Questions

- Should the first version focus on viola specifically or support multiple instruments from day one?
- Should it be for children, adults, or both?
- Should feedback happen live, after recording, or both?
- Should the app include generated accompaniments?
- Should notation be built in early, or should early exercises use simple visual prompts?
- Should human teachers be part of the first release?

## Recommended MVP

Start with a viola-first practice coach:

- Daily lesson dashboard.
- Audio recorder.
- Single-note pitch feedback.
- Rhythm clapback exercises.
- Alto clef note trainer.
- AI teacher chat.
- Session recap and progress tracking.

This keeps the product distinctive, technically achievable, and personal to the workspace name while leaving room to expand into a broader AI music teacher later.
